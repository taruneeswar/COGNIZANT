# AI Terminologies

## Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Simulation of human intelligence by machines.

---

## Machine Learning (ML)

A subset of AI where systems learn from data without explicit programming.

---

## Deep Learning

A branch of ML that uses neural networks with multiple layers.

---

## Generative AI

AI capable of generating new content such as text, images, audio, and code.

---

## Large Language Model (LLM)

A deep learning model trained on large text datasets to understand and generate language.

---

## Prompt

The input or instruction given to an AI model.

---

## Prompt Engineering

The process of designing effective prompts to obtain better AI responses.

---

## Token

The smallest unit of text processed by an LLM.

---

## Embedding

A numerical representation of text that captures semantic meaning.

---

## Vector Database

A specialized database designed to store and search vector embeddings.

---

## Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

A technique that combines external document retrieval with LLMs to improve response quality.

---

## Context Window

The amount of information an LLM can process at one time.

---

## Hallucination

When an AI model generates incorrect or fabricated information.

---

## Fine-Tuning

Training a pre-trained model further on a specific dataset for a specialized task.

---

## AI Agent

An intelligent system capable of planning, reasoning, and using tools to accomplish tasks.

---

## Foundation Model

A large pre-trained model that can be adapted for many downstream tasks.
