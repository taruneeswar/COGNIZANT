# Learning Notes

## What is Agile?

Agile is a software development methodology that focuses on iterative development, collaboration, customer feedback, and continuous improvement.

---

## Agile Manifesto

The Agile Manifesto emphasizes:

- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools.
- Working software over comprehensive documentation.
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation.
- Responding to change over following a plan.

---

## Agile Principles

- Deliver working software frequently.
- Welcome changing requirements.
- Maintain close collaboration with customers.
- Build projects around motivated individuals.
- Continuously improve processes.

---

## Scrum Framework

Scrum is the most widely used Agile framework.

It consists of:

- Roles
- Events
- Artifacts

---

## Scrum Roles

### Product Owner

- Defines product vision.
- Prioritizes Product Backlog.
- Represents customer requirements.

### Scrum Master

- Facilitates Scrum practices.
- Removes impediments.
- Helps the team follow Agile principles.

### Development Team

- Designs, develops, and tests the product.
- Delivers a working product increment every sprint.

---

## Scrum Events

- Sprint
- Sprint Planning
- Daily Scrum
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective

---

## Scrum Artifacts

- Product Backlog
- Sprint Backlog
- Increment

---

## User Story

A User Story describes a feature from the user's perspective.

Example:

As a customer,
I want to log in,
So that I can access my account.

---

## Sprint

A Sprint is a fixed time period (usually 2–4 weeks) during which the Scrum Team completes selected work.

---

## Burndown Chart

A Burndown Chart tracks the remaining work during a sprint and helps monitor progress.

---

## Benefits of Agile

- Faster delivery
- Better customer satisfaction
- Continuous improvement
- Improved teamwork
- Flexibility to changing requirements

---

## Summary

Agile promotes collaboration, adaptability, and continuous delivery of high-quality software through short development cycles called sprints.
